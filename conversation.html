The conversations worth having

Five conversation domains. Each one designed to surface what matters most, not just what's legally required.

What CareGoals covers

What matters most

Most advance care planning tools jump straight to medical preferences — do you want CPR? Ventilator support? But those questions land differently when they're grounded in values first.

Sage starts by learning what a good life looks like for you. Independence. Dignity. Connection to family. Time outdoors. These answers shape everything that follows — and they give your family a richer understanding of who you are, not just what paperwork you've signed.

medical_services

Medical decisions

CPR. Mechanical ventilation. Artificial nutrition. Hospitalization versus comfort care at home. These are the decisions that families face in emergency rooms and ICUs — often without any prior guidance.

Sage walks through these questions gently, in plain language, framing each as a real choice rather than a bureaucratic checkbox. It also captures the reasoning behind the decision — "I don't want to be on a ventilator because I watched my father go through that" is more useful to a family than a checked box.

home

Living and care preferences

Where do you want to live as you age? Home with support, an assisted living community, closer to family in another city? What does your daily routine matter to you? Who should be allowed to visit — and who shouldn't?

These aren't just nice-to-haves. When a parent can no longer speak for themselves, these answers prevent months of difficult family debate — and they allow caregivers to provide genuinely personalized care.

person_check

Who decides

A healthcare proxy is the person who speaks for you when you can't speak for yourself. A durable power of attorney handles financial decisions. These roles are among the most important designations in advance care planning — and they're often undone by conflict when they haven't been explicitly named.

CareGoals helps you think through who you trust most, what guidance you want to give them, and how to notify them so the designation doesn't come as a surprise.

Legacy

What do you want people to remember about you? Are there stories from your life that your grandchildren don't know? Are there values you've tried to live by that you want to pass forward explicitly?

This section is different from the others — it's not about decisions, it's about meaning. Families who complete this section often describe it as the most emotionally significant part. It turns a care planning document into something that endures.

The output — your Care Summary
